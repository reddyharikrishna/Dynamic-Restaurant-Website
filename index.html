Welcome To Indian Foods

Indian food is different from rest of the world not only in taste but also in cooking methods. It reflects a perfect blend of various cultures and ages. Just like Indian culture, food in India has also been influenced by various civilizations, which have contributed their share in its overall development and the present form.

In the southern India, the states make great use of spices, fishes and coconuts, as most of them have coastal kitchens. In the foods of Tamil Nadu use of tamarind is frequently made in order to impart sourness to the dishes. It simply distinguishes the Tamil Food from other cuisines.

Let's Early diet in India mainly consisted of legumes, vegetables, fruits, grains, dairy products, and honey.

Biryani is a celebration of all that is great about Indian food – the heady aromas, the vibrant colours, the fluffy rice and those addictive curry flavours.

Dal is not only an ingredient, but it’s also a traditional, plant-based Indian dish. As an ingredient, dal refers to a split version of legumes like lentils, chickpeas, etc.

Sambar is a South Indian lentil and vegetable stew made with pigeon pea lentils, tamarind and a unique spice blend called sambar powder.

Curious Minds Ask

Which food is best for breakfast India?

RAVA DHOKLA. Steamed semolina cakes garnished with mustard seeds & curry leaves.

What should I order for lunch in India?

Biryani: A flavorful rice dish that is usually made with spices, vegetables, and either chicken, beef, or lamb.

Which language is tiffin?

“Tiffin” is a Punjabi word meaning “snack” or “a little something to eat”. As others have said, it passed into English via the Colonial rulers and their families because it was so useful. It was sometimes used to mean “lunch”.

What is the famous Indian snack?

The Number 1 Indian Snack: Samosa

This deep-fried savoury pastry, filled with spiced mashed potatoes, peas, or lentils, is often referred to as the "king of Indian street food". Whether it's a part of breakfast, a mid-day snack, or an evening treat, the Samosa is a beloved snack across the country.

What is the national snack of India?

What is the national snack of India? India doesn't have a national snack, however, golgappas are top of the mid choice for anyone looking to get a quick snack. They are called puchka in West Bengal, Gupchupp in Andhra Pradesh, Golgappa in Delhi, and Paanipuri everywhere else in the country.
